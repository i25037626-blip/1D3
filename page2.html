About

Anhui

The capital of Anhui Province is Hefei. It is located in eastern China, spanning the Yangtze River and the Huai River. It has a long history and a deep culture. There are famous scenic spots such as Huangshan and Hongcun Xidi, as well as Anhui cuisine, Huangmei opera and other characteristic cultures. It is a province with beautiful scenery and rich humanities.

Food

Stinky Mandarin Fish

Stinky Mandarin Fish is the most recognizable classic dish in Anhui cuisine, which is famous for its unique fermented flavor. After marinating, the fish meat is firm and elastic, in the shape of garlic cloves. After braised, it is salty and fresh. The aroma is mellow, and the smell is slightly special fermentation, but the mouth is fresh and fragrant. The more you taste, the more delicious it is. It is a representative of the food culture in southern Anhui.

Fuliji Roasted Chicken

Fuliji Roasted Chicken is a classic dish in Suzhou, Anhui Province, and one of the "Four Famous Chickens in China". The local chicken is fried first and then marinated. After simmering slowly in the old soup, the color is bright red and oily. The meat is rotten and boneless, fat but not greasy. The aroma is mellow to the bone, salty and delicious. It is a very representative marinated flavor and gift in Anhui.

Culture

Huangmei Opera

Huangmei Opera is a famous local opera of Anhui. It has a gentle and melodic tone, easy to understand and loved by the public. Its classic plays such as The Goddess Marriage and The Female Princess are widely known, making it a bright part of Chinese opera culture.

Xuan Paper

Xuan Paper is a traditional treasure from Jing County, Xuancheng, Anhui. As one of China’s Four Treasures of the Study, it is also a world-class intangible cultural heritage. Made from bamboo bark and rice straw through nearly a hundred manual processes over three years, it is soft, tough, ink-absorbent, and durable. Known as the "thousand-year paper", it is the ideal material for Chinese calligraphy and painting.

Places

Mount Huangshan

Huangshan is a UNESCO World Cultural and Natural Heritage Site in southern Anhui, known as "the most wonderful mountain under heaven". It’s famous for its five wonders: unique pines, strange rocks, sea of clouds, hot springs and winter snow, with steep peaks and stunning misty views.

Hongcun Village

Hongcun is a thousand-year-old Huizhou ancient village at the foot of Huangshan Mountain, Anhui. Built in the shape of an ox with a water system, it has white walls, black tiles and is surrounded by mountains and rivers. With scattered ancient houses, Moon Lake and South Lake, it is known as "Village in Chinese Painting", showing the quiet elegance of Huizhou ancient villages.

Mount Jiuhua

Mount Jiuhua is a famous Buddhist mountain in Chizhou, Anhui, and one of China’s Four Sacred Buddhist Mountains. It has beautiful peaks, clouds all year round, and many ancient temples. The natural scenery and Buddhist culture blend together, quiet and sacred.
